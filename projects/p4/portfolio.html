PARALLAX is a displacement or difference in the apparent position of an object viewed along two different lines of sight, and is measured by the angle or semi-angle of inclination between those two lines. The term is derived from the Greek word parallaxis, meaning “alteration.” Due to foreshortening, nearby objects have a larger parallax than more distant objects when observed from different positions, therefore parallax can be used to determine distances.
