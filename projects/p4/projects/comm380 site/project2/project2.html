G

Gorgeous

Released as a promotional single for reputation, the electropop song debuted at number 13 and features a baby’s voice saying the word “gorgeous.” This baby’s voice is James Reynolds, daughter of Blake Lively and Ryan Reynolds.

H

High Infidelity

From her tenth album, Midnights (3am Edition), “High Infidelity” is a play on words for “High Fidelity” (HiFi) which describes the highest quality of sound. Taylor uses this to describe the worst betrayal you could commit in a relationship: cheating.

I

I Bet You Think About Me (feat. Chris Stapleton) (Taylor's Version) (From The Vault)

From her re-recorded album Red (Taylor’s Version), the country folk-pop ballad was written to be comedic, tongue-in-cheek, and funny. A music video, directed by one of Swift’s close friends Blake Lively in her directorial debut, was also released to accompany the song.

J

Joker and the Queen

From his album =, Ed Sheeran and Taylor Swift duet about love using card game analogies. The piano ballad is about a fulfilling relationship that thrives despite the couple’s fears.

K

Karma

The eleventh track from Taylor Swift’s Midnights, is a playful alt-pop bop with elements of techno and electro-pop. The lyrics discuss Swift’s good karma in comparison to her haters and debuted at number nine on the Billboard Hot 100.

L

Love Story

Released in 2008 as the lead single from Swift’s second album Fearless, Love Story is inspired by the tragedy written by William Shakespeare, Romeo and Juliet is considered one of Swift’s best-selling singles.

M

Me! (feat. Brendon Urie of Panic! at the Disco)

Debuting at #1 in 2019, "Me!" is a cheery, campy bubblegum pop and synth-pop duet, driven by a marching band drumline about embracing one's individuality, with lyrics on self-affirmation and self-love.

N

Nothing New (feat. Phoebe Bridgers)

Written in 2012, but not released until 2021 with Red (Taylor’s Version), "Nothing New" is a guitar-led folk and alternative rock song about anxieties over romance and growing up.

O

Only The Young

Released as a promotional single for Miss Americana, a 2020 Netflix documentary on Taylor Swift, the synth-pop tune was inspired by the 2018 United States elections. Most consider this to be Swift’s most politically-charged song with lyrics addressing matters such as gun violence and mass shootings.

P

Paris

Released as a surprise on Midnights (3am Edition), the lyrics in “Paris” discuss being in love with someone and making imagined adventures out of simple things with them, whilst ignoring other distractions, like gossip and paparazzi and life.

Q

Question...?

The synth-pop tune “Question…?” from Swift’s Midnights (3am Edition) reflects upon a past relationship and confronts the ex-lover with a series of rhetorical questions. The track opens with an interpolation from “Out of the Woods,” from Swift’s 1989 album, saying “I remember.”

R

Red (Taylor's Version)

The fourth studio album from Taylor Swift, the album's title Red refers to the tumultuous, "red" emotions Swift experienced during the album's conception; its songs discuss the complex burning red passion and the conflicting heartbreak felt from love.

S

Snow on the Beach (feat. Lana Del Rey)

In the first collaboration between Taylor Swift and Lana Del Rey, "Snow on the Beach" is a mid-tempo dream pop ballad about the feeling of falling in love with someone at the same time as they are falling in love with you.

T

Teardrops On My Guitar

The country-pop, gentle acoustic guitar-driven ballad released in 2007 on her self-titled debut album peaked at number 13. As Swift’s second single in her career, she received acclaim for her ability to draw on diverse musical styles and her lyrical imagery.

U

Untouchable

"Untouchable" is a cover of rock band Luna Halo's 2007 song with its lyrics and arrangement rewritten by Taylor Swift. Taylor's version of this song is a much slower, softer version than the original.

V

Vigilante Shit

“Vigilante Shit” is built on bubbling trap beats, swirling synths, industrial elements, snare drums, and electric tones. Lyrically, the song is about vengeance declaration, aiming at an enemy and encouraging other women to do the same.

W

We Are Never Ever Getting Back Together

Released in 2012 as the lead single from Red, and eventually hitting number 1 on the Billboard Hot 100, the song is an upbeat incorporation of many pop styles, containing pulsing synthesizers, processed guitar riffs, bass drums, and a spoken-word bridge. Its lyrics express Swift's frustration with an ex-lover who wants to rekindle their relationship.

X

X marks the spot, where we fell apart

A lyric from Swift’s song “Getaway Car” is a synth-pop song with pulsing synthesizers, programmed drums, and distorted vocals. The song's production and intricate lyrics filled with imagery and references to popular culture staples have been praised by critics, many believing this to be the best song on reputation.

Y

You Need To Calm Down

Released as a second single from Swift’s Lover, the upbeat electropop and synth-pop song addresses internet trolls and homophobes, whilst also voicing her support for the LGBTQ+ community. To accompany the song, Swift also urged people to sign the petition for the Equality Act, giving equal rights to all under the law.

Z

Zayn and Taylor Swift - I Don't Wanna Live Forever

Debuting at number 6 and eventually number 2, the sultry electro-R&B and electropop ballad was recorded for the movie Fifty Shades Darker. This is the first collaboration between Zayn and Taylor Swift.
